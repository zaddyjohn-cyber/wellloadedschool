Introduction

Finding a genuine scholarship as a Nigerian student can feel overwhelming — there are thousands of websites, most of them misleading or full of outdated information. In this guide, we've done the research for you.

We've compiled the 10 most reliable and currently active scholarships available to Nigerian students in 2026. Each scholarship has been verified, and we've included direct application links, deadlines, and what you actually need to qualify.

ℹ️
Important Note

Always apply through the official scholarship website. Welloaded Schools does not collect applications or charge any fees. If a website asks you to pay to apply, it is a scam.

How to Apply — Step by Step

Applying for a scholarship can feel like a lot of steps, but when you break it down, it becomes manageable. Here's what to do:

1

Choose the Right Scholarship

Pick a scholarship that matches your field of study, academic level, and nationality. Don't apply to scholarships you don't qualify for.

2

Read the Requirements Carefully

Visit the official scholarship website and read every requirement. Note the deadline and gather the required documents before you start the online form.

3

Prepare Your Documents

Most scholarships require academic transcripts, a personal statement, two reference letters, and a passport copy. Prepare these early.

4

Write a Strong Personal Statement

Your personal statement is your most important document. Explain why you deserve the scholarship, what you plan to do with your education, and how it will benefit your community.

5

Submit Before the Deadline

Submit your application at least 3 days before the deadline. Late submissions are never accepted. Keep a copy of your confirmation email.

Documents You Need

  • International passport (valid for at least 12 months)
  • Academic transcripts (certified copies)
  • Bachelor's degree certificate or NYSC discharge letter
  • Personal statement / statement of purpose
  • Two reference letters from academic supervisors or employers
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) or resume
  • IELTS or TOEFL score (if required)
  • Research proposal (for postgraduate research scholarships)
⚠️
Watch Out for Fake Scholarships

No real scholarship will ask you to pay an application fee. If any website or person asks you to send money to "process your scholarship," it is a scam. Report it immediately.

Tips for a Successful Application

  • Start early — Give yourself at least 4–6 weeks to prepare a strong application.
  • Be honest — Don't exaggerate your achievements. Selection committees can verify information.
  • Tailor your personal statement — Don't use the same essay for every scholarship. Customize it for each one.
  • Get your reference letters early — Ask referees at least 3 weeks before the deadline.
  • Proofread everything — Spelling mistakes and formatting errors can disqualify you.
  • Follow instructions exactly — If the scholarship asks for a 500-word essay, write 500 words — not 200, not 800.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Nigerian students apply for international scholarships? +
Yes. Many international scholarships specifically target students from Nigeria and other African countries, including Commonwealth, Chevening, DAAD, and many others listed in this guide.
Do I need IELTS to apply for foreign scholarships? +
Most scholarships for study in English-speaking countries require IELTS or TOEFL. However, some may waive this requirement if your previous education was conducted entirely in English — check the official scholarship page for details.
How do I know if a scholarship is real or fake? +
Real scholarships: have an official website ending in .gov, .org, or .edu; never charge application fees; provide clear contact information; and are listed on reputable education websites. If a scholarship only appears on WhatsApp or Facebook groups, verify it from the original source before sharing your details.
What is the best scholarship for undergraduate Nigerian students? +
For undergraduates, we recommend looking at the Federal Government Scholarship, PTDF Scholarship, and various country-specific scholarships for Nigerian students. Check our full list above for current options with open deadlines.
Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time? +
Yes, you can and should apply to multiple scholarships. However, if you are awarded more than one, you may need to choose just one — most fully-funded scholarships do not allow you to hold two at the same time. Read each scholarship's terms carefully.
